Teaching

Locke Rowe teaches at both the undergraduate and graduate level in the Department of Ecology & Evolutionary Biology at the University of Toronto.

Graduate Courses

  • EEB 1450H

    Evolutionary Ecology

    Graduate seminar covering foundational and current topics in evolutionary ecology, including life history theory, sexual selection and conflict, ecological speciation, and macro-evolutionary patterns. Readings drawn from primary literature; weekly discussion-based meetings.

  • EEB 1460H

    Sexual Selection and Conflict

    Advanced graduate seminar examining theoretical and empirical work on sexual selection, sexual conflict, and the evolution of reproductive traits. Topics include mate choice, intersexual coevolution, intralocus sexual conflict, and the genetics of sexually antagonistic variation.

Undergraduate Courses

  • EEB 317H / BIO 317H

    Evolutionary Biology

    A third-year course covering core concepts in evolutionary biology, including natural and sexual selection, adaptation, speciation, molecular evolution, and phylogenetics. Emphasis on integrating theory with empirical evidence from diverse organisms.

  • EEB 430H

    Topics in Evolutionary Ecology

    A fourth-year seminar that explores current research questions at the intersection of ecology and evolution. Students read and critically evaluate primary literature, culminating in short presentations and written reviews.
